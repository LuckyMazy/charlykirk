About

A Voice for a Generation

Charles James Kirk (October 14, 1993 — September 10, 2025) was an American conservative political commentator, radio host, author, and activist. He was best known as the founder and president of Turning Point USA, a non-profit organization promoting free-market and constitutionalist principles on college campuses.

Born in Prospect Heights, Illinois, Kirk grew up in the suburbs of Chicago. He became a prominent figure in conservative media and politics, advocating for traditional American values, limited government, and free enterprise.

Career

Timeline

From founding a national organization at 18 to becoming one of America's most-listened-to political voices.

  • 2012 Founded Turning Point USA at age 18, shortly after graduating from Wheeling High School.
  • 2015 Expanded TPUSA's national reach to hundreds of college and high-school chapters across the country.
  • 2017 Published his first book, Time for a Turning Point, and became a nationally recognized commentator.
  • 2019 Launched The Charlie Kirk Show, which became one of the most listened-to conservative podcasts in the United States.
  • 2020 Played a prominent role in the presidential election campaign and founded Turning Point Action.
  • 2022 His podcast topped the charts as one of America's most downloaded political shows with millions of weekly listeners.
  • 2024 Continued as a leading conservative voice during the presidential election cycle, campaigning extensively and broadcasting daily commentary.

Organization

Turning Point USA

Founded by Kirk in 2012, TPUSA's mission is to identify, educate, train, and organize students to promote the principles of free markets, limited government, and American exceptionalism.

The organization operates chapters at thousands of high schools and colleges, hosting AmericaFest — one of the largest annual conservative student conferences in the nation.
